Overview

What this app is for

Blood Sugar Logbook Tracker / Blutzuckertagebuch helps you keep a personal blood sugar diary on your own device. You can record readings, add meal/time context, review history, generate reports, import compatible CSV files, and export backups for your own records.

Medical disclaimer: The app is a tracking and reporting tool only. It does not diagnose, treat, prevent, or cure any medical condition. Always follow your healthcare professional’s advice for diabetes care, medication, insulin dosing, and treatment decisions.

Readings

Logging blood sugar readings

Use the reading entry form to record values consistently. Helpful details include meal timing, remarks, source/device label, and any context that may explain unusual values.

  • Use consistent units and formatting as supported by your installed version.
  • Add notes for meals, exercise, medication timing, illness, or other context.
  • Review entries before saving to avoid incorrect timestamps or duplicate records.
Review

Dashboard and history

The dashboard gives a quick view of your logged information. The history section lets you review, edit, or delete individual records depending on your version.

Tip: If a reading looks incorrect, correct it in the app rather than relying on a later note. Cleaner data makes reports easier to understand.
Import

CSV import

The app may support CSV imports from specific sources, including mapped formats such as Dexcom and OneTouch if those mappings are included in your version.

  • Use CSV files exported directly from the supported source when possible.
  • Do not edit column names unless you know the import format expected by the app.
  • Duplicate handling may compare reading value, date, and time so repeated imports do not create duplicate readings.
  • Source labels or remarks may differ between devices, but duplicate detection should focus on the actual reading record.
Check after import: Always review imported records for date/time accuracy and unit consistency.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is my blood sugar data uploaded to a server?
The app is designed as a local-first logbook. Your readings are normally stored on your device unless you export, share, email, upload, or back them up yourself.
Can I use this as medical advice?
No. The app is a tracking tool. Consult a qualified healthcare professional for diagnosis, treatment, medication, insulin dosing, and emergency decisions.
What should I do before deleting app data?
Export a backup or PDF/CSV report first if you need to keep a copy. Once app data is cleared, local records may not be recoverable.
Are Dexcom and OneTouch CSV files supported?
Your current app history indicates Dexcom and OneTouch CSV mapping were the known supported formats. Always test with a small file and review imported results after import.
